Career Decision Framework

Evaluate career opportunities based on comprehensive factors beyond compensation. This framework helps you assess the true value of a role by measuring growth potential, impact, and long-term career development.

Important Instructions

It's important to evaluate all career opportunities on all of the short- and long-term factors that drive on-the-job motivation and satisfaction. This form summarizes these eight factors. Too often candidates make short term decisions based on current circumstances, rather than looking at the complete picture. Completing this form objectively will let candidates fully understand the career merits of any job opportunity. If you don't have enough information to make this assessment, be sure to get it before accepting or rejecting an offer.

Framework Insights

The 30% Solution Principle

A true career move should provide at least 30% non-monetary increase through stretch assignments, impact, growth opportunities, and satisfaction - even if the salary increase is modest.

Long-Term Career Success

When candidates evaluate opportunities using this framework, over 90% remain satisfied with their decision after the first year, creating sustainable career progression.

Beyond Compensation

Most career decisions focus too heavily on immediate compensation. Successful professionals think long-term about career trajectory and skill development.

Information Gathering

Before accepting any offer, gather comprehensive information about challenges, performance metrics, and team dynamics to make an informed decision.

Benchmark Your Experience

Score your best previous role using this framework as a benchmark. Don't accept opportunities that score significantly lower, regardless of compensation.
