Methodology Rankings

▼

1. Performance-based Hiring (Lou Adler) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 95/100

Key Strengths:

  • Automatic conversion of job descriptions into performance objectives via custom GPT
  • Mathematical formula for predicting quality of hire (Ability × Fit × MotivationN)
  • Bi-directional assessment ensuring mutual success
  • 8-step structured interview with specific time allocations
  • Comprehensive Quality of Hire Scorecard with evidence-based scoring
  • AI integration for job descriptions, resume evaluation, and scorecard completion
  • Post-hire validation system for continuous improvement
  • Focus on "Win-Win Hiring" with 30% job stretch

Unique Differentiators:

  • Only system that starts with performance-based job analysis
  • Creates the missing bridge between pre-hire assessment and post-hire success
  • Only methodology that treats motivation as an exponential factor
  • "Need More Evidence" (NME) protocol prevents premature decisions
  • Measures candidates against actual performance objectives, not job descriptions
  • Built-in bias control through first impression measurement
  • Continuous validation loop using same scorecard post-hire

Best For: All positions from entry-level to executive, particularly valuable for critical roles

2. Topgrading (Brad Smart) ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 75/100

Key Strengths:

  • Extremely thorough chronological career review
  • TORC (Threat of Reference Check) encourages candidate honesty
  • Strong track record of improving hiring success rates
  • Comprehensive 12-step process
  • Excellent at identifying behavior patterns

Limitations:

  • Time-intensive (3-4 hour interviews)
  • Can overwhelm candidates and interviewers
  • Less focus on future potential
  • No systematic approach to candidate evaluation of opportunity
  • Lacks post-hire validation mechanism

Best For: Senior executive positions where hiring mistakes are extremely costly

3. WHO Method (Geoff Smart & Randy Street) ⭐⭐⭐½ 70/100

Key Strengths:

  • Four distinct interview types for comprehensive assessment
  • Strong scorecard methodology
  • Excellent reference checking process
  • More accessible than Topgrading
  • Clear implementation structure

Limitations:

  • Can feel mechanical and rigid
  • Multiple rounds may exhaust candidates
  • Limited focus on motivation measurement
  • No bi-directional assessment
  • Less sophisticated than PbH in predicting success

Best For: Organizations wanting structure without Topgrading's intensity

4. Behavioral Interviewing (STAR Method) ⭐⭐⭐ 55/100

Key Strengths:

  • Easy to train interviewers
  • Based on solid premise of past behavior predicting future
  • Widely understood and accepted
  • Provides concrete examples
  • Adaptable to any industry

Limitations:

  • Candidates can rehearse responses
  • Insufficient as standalone method
  • Doesn't measure potential or adaptability
  • No systematic scoring mechanism
  • Favors good storytellers over performers

Best For: Entry-level positions or as component of larger methodology

The Game-Changing Innovation: Bi-Directional Assessment

Performance-based Hiring's bi-directional approach represents a paradigm shift in hiring:

Traditional Model: Company → Candidate (one-way)
PbH Model: Company ⟷ Candidate (mutual evaluation)

This innovation reduces turnover, increases engagement, creates true "Win-Win" outcomes, and focuses on long-term career success, not just job placement.
