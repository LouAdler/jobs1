Role Overview

As the Senior HR Business Partner, you will function as a strategic consultant to senior leadership, leveraging your expertise to design and execute people-centric strategies that fuel business growth and organizational effectiveness. This role is pivotal in shaping a high-performing, innovative culture by leading significant organizational development, talent management, and change initiatives. You will act as a coach to leaders, a driver of transformation, and a key partner in translating high-level business objectives into actionable workforce and talent plans, ensuring the People function is a core enabler of Booking.com's success.

Why This Opportunity Matters

Purpose

Your work will directly enable Booking.com's mission to make it easier for everyone to experience the world. By architecting a high-performing and engaged organization, you ensure our teams are positioned to innovate and execute, ultimately shaping a more inclusive and seamless travel experience for millions of customers globally.

Growth

This is a career-defining opportunity to operate at a senior strategic level, leading complex, high-impact organizational transformations. You will sharpen your executive coaching capabilities, deepen your expertise in organizational design, and gain unparalleled experience in driving change within a large-scale, data-driven tech environment, preparing you for future executive HR leadership roles.

Motivators

For a strategic HR leader who thrives on influence and impact, this role offers the chance to partner with and coach senior executives, shaping the direction of the business. You will have the autonomy to design and implement cutting-edge talent and organizational strategies, see the tangible results of your work on business outcomes, and be a key player in a dynamic, fast-paced, and innovative culture.

Major Performance Objectives

1Develop a Strategic People Plan

Within the first 90 days, partner with the assigned business unit's senior leadership team to develop and launch a comprehensive, 12-month strategic people plan. This plan must align directly with the top 3-5 business priorities for the upcoming fiscal year. Success involves conducting a thorough needs analysis, gaining leadership consensus on all initiatives, and establishing key performance indicators (KPIs) for each people-focused objective. The final plan should outline specific actions for talent management, organizational design, and culture that will be implemented in the subsequent quarters.

2Lead Organizational Design and Transformation

By the end of the first year, lead one major organizational design and operating model evolution for a key department within the business unit. This initiative must address identified inefficiencies and be designed to improve team productivity and effectiveness by at least 15% against baseline metrics. The project will involve facilitating leadership alignment, mapping future-state organizational structures, defining new role responsibilities, and creating and executing a comprehensive change management plan to ensure successful adoption and minimal business disruption.

3Implement an Executive Talent and Succession Framework

Within the first six months, design and implement a new talent review and succession planning framework for the Executive Leadership Team (ELT+). This program must identify all critical roles, map high-potential talent across the business unit, and result in a validated succession pipeline covering at least 75% of critical leadership positions. The process should include facilitating calibration sessions with senior leadership and creating individual development plans for high-potential successors to ensure readiness for future leadership transitions.

4Drive Cultural Transformation Initiatives

Over the course of the first year, drive at least two significant cultural transformation initiatives aimed at embedding a customer-centric, high-performance culture. Utilizing organizational health and engagement metrics, you will identify key areas for improvement and launch targeted programs. Success will be measured by a 10% improvement in related engagement scores and the successful adoption of new cultural behaviors and practices by 80% of the leadership team as measured through quarterly performance reviews and 360-degree feedback.

Critical First-Year Subtasks

Conduct a 30-60 Day Operational Audit

Within the first 60 days, conduct a comprehensive audit of the business unit's existing people strategies, organizational structure, talent pipeline, and cultural health metrics. Present a findings report to the leadership team that includes a SWOT analysis and outlines the most critical short-term and long-term priorities to inform the strategic people plan.

Co-lead Strategic Workforce Planning

In the first 90-180 days, collaborate with Finance and Business Operations to co-lead the BU's strategic workforce planning cycle. This involves analyzing future business needs, identifying critical skill gaps, and developing a comprehensive plan for talent acquisition, development, and retention to support long-term business goals.

Establish Executive Coaching Cadence

By the end of the first quarter, establish a structured coaching and advising cadence with each member of the senior leadership team. These sessions will focus on enhancing their leadership effectiveness, managing team performance, and driving organizational change, with progress measured by semi-annual stakeholder feedback.

Manage the Talent and Performance Cycle

Within the first year, successfully manage the end-to-end talent review and performance management process for the business unit. This includes training leaders on best practices, facilitating calibration sessions to ensure fairness and consistency, and translating the outputs into actionable succession and development plans.

Launch a DEI and Wellbeing Program

By the end of a year, partner with the Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) and Wellbeing teams to launch at least one new program tailored to the business unit's specific needs. Success will be measured by the program's participation rate and its impact on inclusion and wellbeing scores in pulse surveys.

Facilitate Cross-Functional HR Collaboration

On an ongoing basis, establish and lead a monthly sync with HR Centers of Excellence (CoEs) such as Learning & Development and Total Rewards. The goal is to ensure integrated, seamless HR solutions are delivered to the business, tracking the successful joint delivery of at least three major initiatives per year.

Continuously Evaluate and Integrate AI to Improve Performance

Within the first 90-180 days, take ownership of identifying how AI and automation tools can support or enhance the core responsibilities of this role. This includes researching and proposing applications for AI in areas like organizational sentiment analysis, talent mapping, and predictive workforce planning to drive more data-driven HR strategies.

This is a role for a resilient and proactive leader who sees challenges as opportunities and is passionate about driving organizational change and building high-performing teams.
