Key Recommendations

1. Adopt Performance-based Hiring

Define roles by the results a person needs to achieve, not a list of skills. This forces clarity, eliminates the expectation-reality gap, and breaks the "Disengaged Leader Cascade" by ensuring managers are hired for leadership ability.

Add Two Pre-Offer Safety Checks:
  1. Ask the candidate: "Forget the compensation for a moment, why do you want this job and why do you think if represents a good career move?" If the answer is vague or general and not about the work itself, don't make an offer.
  2. Ask the hiring manager: "What does the person need to do in this role to be considered a strong performer and why is the person both qualified and motivated to do this work?" If the evidence is vague, emotional, or filled with hyperbole, don't approve the offer.

Potential Impact: Higher quality of hire, drastic reduction in early turnover, and a clear foundation for onboarding.

2. Engineer Onboarding

Design distinct, 90-day onboarding journeys for professionals and managers to improve retention by up to 82%.

3. Enable New Managers

Provide leadership training *before* or immediately upon promotion to break the "Disengaged Leader Cascade."

4. Systematize Feedback

Implement frequent recognition and mandate weekly check-ins for new hires to build rapport and clarify expectations.

5. Activate Career Paths

Discuss growth opportunities from Day 1 to show long-term investment in high-potential talent.
