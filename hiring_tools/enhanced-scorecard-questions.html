Performance-based Hiring Methodology

Core Principle: The best way to assess competencies is to have candidates describe specific accomplishments that demonstrate each factor being evaluated. Look for patterns of achievement and growth over time.

  • Ask for Examples: "Can you tell me about a major accomplishment that best demonstrates [specific competency]?"
  • Probe for Details: Dig deeper with fact-finding questions to understand the full context and the candidate's actual role
  • Look for Trends: Examine how behaviors and skills have evolved and improved throughout their career
  • Verify Claims: Use follow-up questions to ensure authenticity and depth of experience

WORK HISTORY REVIEW

Work History Review
ðŸ’¡ Interview Questions & Probing Techniques
Primary Questions:
"Please walk me through your career progression, starting with your most recent position. For each role, tell me about your responsibilities, key accomplishments, and reasons for moving on."
"Looking at your career trajectory, what would you say has been the common thread or pattern in the roles you've chosen?"
Probing Questions:
"What specific factors led you to make each career transition?"
Follow up: "Did you achieve what you hoped for in that move?"
"In which roles did you feel you grew the most professionally? What enabled that growth?"
"Looking back, are there any career decisions you would make differently? Why?"
Evidence to Look For: Logical progression, increasing responsibilities, strategic career moves, ability to articulate decisions, pattern of being recruited or promoted, consistency in career narrative.

Primary Questions:
